Do you want to finalise your divorce?

Your marriage will be legally ended after the final order is made. This might affect your finances.

! Warning
If you have not yet finished any negotiations or legal proceedings about your money, property or other assets then you should seek legal advice before finalising your divorce. If you want to settle your finances first, then save and sign out.
Read more about settling your finances before finalising your divorce

Your financial rights will change after the divorce is finalised. If you have not settled your finances then you should save and sign out and seek legal advice.

Your husband or wife has already confirmed this joint application. If you do not confirm then they can still apply to finalise the divorce as a sole applicant. This will mean you will become the respondent and the divorce could be finalised without your confirmation. They must give you 14 days notice if they intend to do this.

If you want to settle your finances before the divorce is finalised then you can apply to delay the final order. You can do this by filling out a ‘general application’ D11 form and sending it to the court. You only need to apply to delay the final order if you receive notice from your husband or wife that they are going to apply to finalise the divorce as a sole applicant.

Changing to a sole application

Your husband or wife has already confirmed the application for a final order. The quickest way for you to finalise your divorce is for you to also confirm at the bottom of this page.

If you want to change to a sole application then it will delay the divorce. You can change by following the steps below:

  1. Download and fill out an application for a final order (as a sole applicant).
  2. Give notice to your husband or wife that you are intending to apply for a final order as a sole applicant. You can do this by sending them a draft copy of the application by email or post. You will need to fill out a ‘certificate of service’ form to prove to the court that you have given them notice.
  3. You have to give your husband or wife 14 days to respond, starting from the day they are ‘served’ the application. After 14 days, then send the following documents and evidence to the court:
    • The application for a Final Order (as a sole applicant)
    • The ‘certificate of service’ which proves that you have given notice to your husband or wife that you are intending to apply for a final order as a sole applicant.

You can either post or email the documents and evidence to the court. Details of where to send them are on any correspondence you have received from the court.
