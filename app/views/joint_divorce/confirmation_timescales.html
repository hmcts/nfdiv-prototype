What happens next

Your joint application will be checked by court staff. You and your {{ data['husband-wife'] }} will receive an email notification by confirming whether it has been accepted.

If it’s accpeted, then the next step is for you to confirm that you want the application to continue by applying for a ‘conditional order’. You have to wait 20 weeks until you can apply.

You can use the time between now and then to decide how the money and property is divided. You can also make arrangements for children, if you have them. Dividing money and property is dealt with separately to the divorce process.

Dividing your money and property

It’s usually more straightforward and less expensive if you agree with your {{ data['husband-wife'] }} on how to divide your money and property. Get help agreeing.

If you do agree then you can make the agreement legally binding. This is known as asking the court to make a ‘consent order’.

If you disagree then you can ask the court to decide for you. This is known as applying for a ‘financial order’.

Read the guidance on money and property when you divorce or separate.

If you need help

Court staff can give you help with your application. They cannot give you legal advice. You should speak to a solicitor or legal adviser.
