How to finalise your divorce

The quickest way to finalise your divorce is to get your husband or wife to confirm your joint application for a final order. They have been emailed details of how to do this.

If they will not do this then you can apply to finalise your divorce as a sole applicant.

  1. Download and fill out an application for a final order (as a sole applicant).
  2. Give notice to your husband or wife that you are intending to apply for a final order as a sole applicant. You can do this by sending them a draft copy of the application by email or post. You should keep evidence that you have sent it.
  3. Wait 2 weeks, to give them time to respond to the notice that you are intending to serve them.
  4. After 2 weeks, then ‘serve’ (send) them the application for a final order. You will need to prove to the court that you have served them the application. You could use a private professional to serve them, such as a ‘process server’. You can find a process server online.
  5. Send the following documents and evidence to the court:
    • The application for a Final Order (as a sole applicant)
    • Proof that you have given notice that you intend to serve the application for a Final Order on your husband or wife. For example, the email or letter you sent them with a draft copy of the application.
    • A statement confirming that you have given notice to your husband or wife that you are intending to serve them the application for a final order. The statement should also confirm that at least 14 days has passed between notifying them of your intention to serve and actually ‘serving’ them the application for a final order.
    • The application for a Final Order (as a sole applicant)
    • A ‘certificate of service’ which proves that the application for a Final Order was served on your husband or wife.

You can either post or email the documents and evidence to the court. Details of where to send them are on any correspondence you have received from the court.
