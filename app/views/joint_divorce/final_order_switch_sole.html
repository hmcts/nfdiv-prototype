How to finalise your divorce

The quickest way to finalise your divorce is to ask your husband or wife to confirm your joint application for a final order. They have been emailed details of how to do this.

If they will not confirm then you can apply for a final order as a sole applicant. To do this you need to follow these steps:

  1. Download and fill out an application for a final order (as a sole applicant).
  2. Give notice to your husband or wife that you are intending to apply for a final order as a sole applicant. You can do this by sending them a draft copy of the application by email or post. You will need to fill out a ‘certificate of service’ form to prove to the court that you have given them notice.
  3. Wait 14 days to give your husband or wife time to respond. Then upload the following documents:
    • The application for a Final Order (as a sole applicant)
    • The ‘certificate of service’ which proves that you have given notice to your husband or wife that you are intending to apply for a final order as a sole applicant.

Document upload

Make sure the photo or scan shows the whole document. Check you can read all the text before uploading it. If court staff cannot read it then it may be rejected.
You can take a picture with your phone and upload it
  1. Place your document on a flat service in a well-lit room. Use a flash if you need to.
  2. Take a picture of the whole document. You should be able to see its edges.
  3. Check you can read all the writing, including the handwriting.
  4. Email or send the photo or scan to the device you are using now.
  5. Upload it using the link below.

You should upload at least one clear image which shows the whole document. If you think it will help court staff read the details you can upload more images. If your document has more than one page then you should upload at least one image of each page.
