You can use the English or Welsh courts to get a divorce

Your answers indicate that you can apply for a divorce in England or Wales because both of you are 'habitually resident'.

Read more about habitual residence
If your life is mainly based in England or Wales then you're what is legally known as 'habitually resident'.
This may include working, owning property, having children in school, or your main family life taking place in England or Wales.
The examples above aren't a complete list of what makes up habitual residence, and just because some of them apply to you doesn't mean you're habitually resident. If you're not sure, you should get legal advice.
