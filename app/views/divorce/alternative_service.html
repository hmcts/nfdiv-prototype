How to proceed with your divorce

The court usually needs to hear from both parties in a marriage before it can grant a divorce. It’s therefore important that your husband or wife responds to your application.


! Warning
The simplest way to proceed is for you to contact your husband or wife and ask them to respond, if it’s safe to do so. They can still respond, even though the deadline has passed.

If they still do not respond then you can choose one of the following options to progress your application, depending on your situation.

Which situation applies to you?

I have another email address or postal address for my husband or wife

Provide their new email address or postal address to the court. You can phone, email or post it using the contact details in the ‘Getting help’ section at the top of this page.

Postal addresses can be UK or international. It can be their home address or their solicitor’s address. You should avoid providing their work address but if you have to then you should ask their permission first.

You do not have to pay anything to update their email or postal address.

I have their email address but not their postal address

If you have their email address but not their postal address, then you can apply to the court to only send the application to their email address. This application costs an additional £50, unless you are eligible for Help With Fees.

How to apply: Download and complete form D11. Send the form to the Courts and Tribunals Service Centre by email or post.

Email: contactdivorce@justice.gov.uk

I need to search government records for my husband’s or wife’s postal address

You can apply to search government records for your husband or wife’s address, for example the electoral register. They must live in England or Wales. The application costs an additional £50, unless you are eligible for Help With Fees.

How to apply: Call the Courts and Tribunals Service Centre on 0300 303 0642 and ask for the form to be sent to you.

How to pay: Call the Courts and Tribunals Service Centre on 0300 303 0642 to make the £50 payment by card (between 8.30am and 5pm). If you're eligible for Help With Fees then you'll need to apply again and get a new reference number.

I think my husband or wife is receiving the application but is choosing not to respond

If you think that your husband or wife is deliberately not responding then you can pay a professional to deliver (serve) the divorce papers to them. You could either use a ‘process server’ (a privately-hired professional) or a court bailiff (an employee of the court).

If the application is delivered successfully, you'll be given a letter as evidence that your husband or wife has received the application. This is known as an ‘certificate of service'.

If your husband or wife does not respond within 14 days of getting the application, you can use this certificate to continue your divorce without their response.

Serving using a process server (a privately hired professional)

Search online for ‘process servers’ or contact a local solicitor who should be able to give you the contact details for a local company.

Serving using the court bailiffs

The bailiff service can only be used at addresses in England and Wales where delivery by post has already failed. If you have a new address for your husband or wife, you’ll have to get it delivered by post to that address, before you can apply for the bailiff service. Using the court bailiff service costs an additional £110, unless you are eligible for Help With Fees.

How to apply: Download and complete form D89. You'll also need to send:

  • a photo of your husband or wife
  • a copy of your divorce application

Send the completed form to to the Courts and Tribunals Service Centre by email or post:

I have evidence that my husband or wife has received the application, but will not or cannot respond

If you know your husband or wife has received the application, but will not or cannot respond, you can apply to the court for a ‘deemed service’. This means that the court will decide that your husband or wife has received the application, and that you can continue without their response. This application costs an additional £50, unless you are eligible for Help With Fees.

You will need to be able to prove that your husband or wife received the application. For example, you have a text message from your husband or wife saying they have received it

I’ve tried every possible way of delivering the application

Under exceptional circumstances, you can ask the court to ‘dispense with service’. This means they decide your husband or wife does not have to be served (sent) the divorce application. This application costs an additional £50, unless you are eligible for Help With Fees.

It’s up to the court to decide whether to grant this. Before it can be considered, you’ll need to show that you’ve done everything you can to find them by contacting their:

  • relatives (including children)
  • friends
  • last known employer
  • trade union or professional organisation

You'll also need to have tried to find their address by searching for it on government databases.

How to apply: Download and complete form D11 and form D13b. Send the forms to the Courts and Tribunals Service Centre by email or post:
