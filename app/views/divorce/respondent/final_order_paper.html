Finalising your divorce

It’s usually quicker and easier if your husband or wife applies for the final order. This is because they are the applicant. You can save and sign out and ask them to apply, if it’s safe to do so.

If your husband or wife will not or cannot apply to finalise the divorce, then you can. You will need to apply for permission because you are the ‘respondent’ in the divorce process.

! Warning
If you have not finished negotiations about your money, property or other assets then you should seek legal advice before applying to finalise your divorce

If you want to apply for the final order, to legally end your marriage then follow these steps:

  1. Download and fill out a D11 ‘general application’ form
    This is to ask the court for permission to apply to end your marriage as the respondent.
  2. Download and fill out a D36 ‘application for a final order’ form
    This is to apply for a final order, and end your marriage
  3. Upload both forms below and submit them to the court

Document upload

Make sure the photo or scan shows the whole document. Check you can read all the text before uploading it. If court staff cannot read it then it may be rejected.
You can take a picture with your phone and upload it
  1. Place your document on a flat service in a well-lit room. Use a flash if you need to.
  2. Take a picture of the whole document. You should be able to see its edges.
  3. Check you can read all the writing, including the handwriting.
  4. Email or send the photo or scan to the device you are using now.
  5. Upload it using the link below.

You should upload at least one clear image which shows the whole document. If you think it will help court staff read the details you can upload more images. If your document has more than one page then you should upload at least one image of each page.
