Dividing your money and property

It’s important that you settle how to divide your money and property before the end of the divorce process. Otherwise you or your husband could make financial claims against each other, after the marriage is ended. Even if you have kept your finances separate during the marriage. The way to settle your finances is by applying to the court for a ‘financial order’.

A financial order is a legal document that describes how you are going to split your money, property, pensions and other assets. You need a financial order whether you can reach agreement with your husband or whether you want the court to decide for you.

You need to apply for a financial order separately to the divorce application. You will pay an additional fee when you apply (unless you are eligible for help with fees). The court will deal with it separately to the main divorce process. You will probably need legal advice when you apply for a financial order.

For legal reasons, the court needs confirmation now that you want to apply. You will receive an email with more information after you have submitted this application for divorce.

It’s important to have a financial order in place by the time your divorce is finalised. Otherwise you or your husband could make claims on each other’s finances after the marriage is ended. Even if you have kept your finances separate during the marriage. If you select yes, then you do not have to go ahead with the application or pay any additional fees. It just gives you the option to apply later in the process.
